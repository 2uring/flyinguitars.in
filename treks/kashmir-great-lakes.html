Trek 01

Kashmir Great Lakes

Route
Sonamarg to Naranag
Distance
~72 km
Duration
7 days
Max Altitude
~4,200 m
Times Done
Twice

There are treks you do once and remember fondly. And then there's Kashmir Great Lakes — the one that pulls you back. I've done this trek twice now, and each time it felt like a completely different journey. Not because the trail changed, but because I did.

The trail

The trek begins at Sonamarg — the "Meadow of Gold" — where the Sindh river cuts through a wide, grassy valley framed by snow-dusted peaks. From here, you climb through dense pine forests, cross glacial streams, and ascend into a high-altitude corridor that connects seven alpine lakes, each a different shade of impossible blue and green.

Vishansar, Kishansar, Gadsar, Satsar, Gangabal, Nundkol — the names alone carry a rhythm. The lakes sit between 3,600 and 4,200 metres, nestled in valleys where wildflowers blanket the ground in July and August. On clear days, the reflections in the water are so perfect they make you question which way is up.

Why twice

The first time, I went for the lakes. The second time, I went for everything in between — the silence of the passes at dawn, the way the light shifts over Gadsar Pass in the late afternoon, the feeling of sleeping at 4,000 metres and waking up to a sky you can't photograph justice for.

The first time you go for the destination. The second time you go for the journey. Both times, the mountains give you exactly what you need.

The descent

The trail ends at Naranag, a small village with an ancient temple and the quiet satisfaction of having walked through one of the most beautiful landscapes on earth. Seven days. Seventy-two kilometres. Seven lakes. And a head full of images that no camera can fully capture — though I tried, both times.
