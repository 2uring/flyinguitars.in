About

Engineer by training.
Artist by nature.

I'm Faiz Haq — currently pursuing my Masters at IIT Hyderabad, where the rigour of engineering meets the freedom of creative expression. Under the alias flyinguitars, I explore two parallel worlds: the raw, emotive language of the electric guitar, and the quiet poetry of light captured through a lens.

When I'm not in the lab or the lecture hall, you'll find me crafting tones through Neural DSP's Archetype Asato and Archetype Rabea, chasing golden hour across campus, or losing myself in the infinite feedback loop between music and visual art.

Music

Tone is
everything.

The electric guitar is my first language. From ambient swells and soaring leads to tight, percussive riffs — every note is shaped with intention. I believe tone isn't just about gear; it's about feel, dynamics, and the space between the notes.

My signal chain runs through Neural DSP, where two plugins form the backbone of my sound. Whether I'm recording at home, jamming, or producing content for YouTube — these are the tools that translate what I hear in my head into what the world hears through the speakers.

Plugin
Archetype: Tim Henson
Pristine cleans, crystalline leads, and modern high-gain — built for precision and expression.
Plugin
Archetype: Rabea
Warm crunch, massive rhythm tones, and ambient post-rock textures in a single suite.
Photography

Chasing light,
one frame at a time.

Photography, for me, started with skies — those fleeting moments when the clouds arrange themselves into something you'll never see again. It grew into a practice of patience: waiting for the right light, the right shadow, the right silence in a frame.

From golden hour landscapes and moody street frames to candid portraits — my work lives across two handles. @faiskies_ for the personal, contemplative eye. @flyinguitars for where music and visuals converge.
